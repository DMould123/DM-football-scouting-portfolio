Scout Report

This was my first opportunity to watch Gessime Yassine live following his impressive displays at international level with Morocco U-20, where he was a key contributor in the team’s World Cup triumph in Chile.

Operating primarily from the left side, Yassine immediately stood out for his confidence in 1v1 situations. He consistently looked to engage his direct opponent and was not hesitant to carry the ball at pace into advanced areas.

Up against on-loan Chelsea defender Ben Chilwell, Yassine caused persistent problems through his ability to dribble both inside and outside. He showed excellent balance and close control, repeatedly committing the defender and forcing him into reactive defending.

A clear example of his effectiveness came in the build-up to the penalty he won. Driving directly at Chilwell, Yassine shifted the ball sharply across his body, created separation, and drew the foul inside the penalty area. This action highlighted his bravery in possession and composure in decisive moments.

Given his age, current output, and continued development, Yassine appears well-positioned for a significant move in the upcoming summer window.
